\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

\subsection{Experiment Setup}
\label{sec:experiment_setup}

\subsubsection{Datasets}
\label{sec:datasets}

We evaluate \method{} on two publicly available 3D chest CT benchmarks.
\textbf{CT-RATE}~\cite{hamamci2024ct2rep} provides 3D chest CT volumes paired with free-text radiology reports covering a broad spectrum of thoracic findings.
\textbf{RadGenome-ChestCT}~\cite{zhang2024radgenome} extends CT-RATE with structured, anatomically annotated radiology reports, enabling finer-grained spatial evaluation.
From each dataset we sample 2{,}500 cases and apply an 80/10/10 train/valid/test split (seed$=$42), yielding 2{,}000/250/250 cases per source.
Four volumes with corrupted NIfTI headers are excluded, resulting in 496 test patients totalling 3{,}979 sentences.
All volumes are loaded via SimpleITK, intensity-normalized to $[0,1]$, and trilinearly resampled to $128{\times}128{\times}128$ voxels.


\subsubsection{Baselines}
\label{sec:baselines}

We compare \method{} against representative 3D CT report generation methods that span three design paradigms.
\textbf{CT2Rep}~\cite{hamamci2024ct2rep} decodes reports from a dense 3D visual prefix in a standard encoder--decoder architecture.
\textbf{CT-AGRG}~\cite{dipiazza2024ctagrg} augments the visual prefix with an anatomical graph module that incorporates organ-level structural priors.
\textbf{3D-CT-GPT}~\cite{chen2024_3dctgpt} adapts autoregressive language modeling to 3D CT by projecting volumetric features into the GPT token space.
\textbf{MedVInT}~\cite{zhang2024medvint} fine-tunes a vision--language model for 3D medical visual question answering and report generation.
\textbf{Reg2RG}~\cite{chen2025reg2rg} introduces region-level grounding by aligning visual features with anatomical regions, providing the closest existing comparison in terms of spatial awareness.
As a cross-modal reference from the 2D domain, we include \textbf{MAIRA-2}~\cite{bannur2024maira2}, a grounded chest X-ray report generation system that optionally exposes sentence-level bounding-box evidence.

A key distinction is that none of the 3D baselines produce per-sentence cited evidence objects in their output interface.
Their evidence (if any) is consumed internally and not exposed for post-hoc spatial verification.
This makes them structurally incompatible with deterministic verifier rules such as \textsc{CLEAR}-6, and prevents direct measurement of spatial citation faithfulness.


\subsubsection{Implementation Details}
\label{sec:implementation}

\noindent\textbf{Frozen components.}
The 3D visual encoder is a SwinUNETR~\cite{hatamizadeh2022swinunetr} backbone (62M parameters) from MONAI~\cite{cardoso2022monai}, pretrained on BTCV via self-supervised learning~\cite{tang2022selfsupervised}.
The encoder produces a 48-dimensional feature map from each $128^3$ input volume and is kept entirely frozen throughout all experiments.
The text encoder is all-MiniLM-L6-v2~\cite{reimers2019sentencebert,wang2020minilm} (22M parameters, 384-dimensional output), also frozen.
The octree splitter and sentence planner are deterministic rule-based modules with zero trainable parameters.

\noindent\textbf{Trainable component.}
The only learned parameter is the cross-modal projection matrix $\mathbf{W}_{\mathrm{proj}}\in\mathbb{R}^{384\times 768}$ (295K parameters).
It is optimized with an InfoNCE contrastive objective~\cite{oord2018cpc} using AdamW (learning rate $10^{-3}$, weight decay $10^{-4}$, cosine annealing schedule) for up to 50 epochs with early stopping (patience$=$10) on validation loss.
Training data consists of (sentence, cited-token) pairs extracted from anatomy-primary routing traces on the 4{,}000-case training split.
The projection is initialized orthogonally and converges in approximately 30 minutes on cached token features (no backpropagation through the 3D encoder).

\noindent\textbf{Language model.}
For sentence generation (Stage~3c) and LLM-based judging (Stage~5a), we use Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct in bfloat16 precision with temperature$=$0.3 and a maximum of 256 generated tokens.
The LLM receives textualized evidence (token coordinates, octree levels, and Evidence Card constraints) as a structured prompt; no visual embeddings are injected into the LLM hidden states.

\noindent\textbf{Shared hyperparameters.}
All seven ablation configurations share: token bank size $B{=}128$, per-sentence citation budget $k{=}8$, spatial IoU weight $\lambda_{\mathrm{spatial}}{=}0.3$, routing IoU threshold $\tau_{\mathrm{IoU}}{=}0.04$, boundary blending coefficient $\beta{=}0.1$, R1 same-side threshold $\rho_{\min}{=}0.6$, R2 anatomy support ratio$=$0.8, and input volume size $128^3$.
R4 (size plausibility) and R5 fallback lexicon are disabled in all reported configurations.

\noindent\textbf{Evaluation metrics.}
We report three complementary layers of metrics.
(1)~\emph{Spatial consistency}: the sentence-level violation rate (Viol\%), defined as the fraction of sentences triggering at least one \textsc{CLEAR}-6 rule, together with per-rule violation counts for R1 (laterality), R3 (depth), and R6b (cross-sentence presence conflict).
Patient-level 95\% confidence intervals are obtained via paired bootstrap ($R{=}5{,}000$ resamples) with Holm step-down correction for multiple comparisons.
(2)~\emph{NLG quality}: BLEU-4~\cite{papineni2002bleu}, ROUGE-L~\cite{lin2004rouge}, and METEOR~\cite{banerjee2005meteor}, computed at the sentence level and averaged across the test set.
Since the planner uses reference report sentences as topics, NLG scores serve as \emph{control variables} that verify evidence gating does not degrade text quality, rather than as primary contribution metrics.
(3)~\emph{Grounding metrics}: mean anatomy overlap (IoU between cited tokens and the target anatomy region), Hit@$k$ (whether at least one top-$k$ token overlaps ${\geq}50\%$ with the anatomy), routing accuracy, laterality accuracy, citation faithfulness (CF: whether the generated laterality claim agrees with cited token positions), and depth faithfulness (DF: whether the generated depth description matches the cited token levels).


\subsection{Performance Comparison}
\label{sec:performance}

Table~\ref{tab:comparison} positions \method{} relative to existing 3D CT report generators along two axes: the provenance interface taxonomy (left columns) and shared NLG metrics (right columns).
We classify each method by four output-level properties: citation granularity (whether per-sentence evidence objects are exposed), evidence commitment (whether evidence is mandatory or optional), verification (whether deterministic spatial rules can be applied), and repair (whether closed-loop correction is supported).

All existing 3D CT generators consume visual features internally but do not expose sentence-conditioned cited 3D evidence objects in their output.
\method{} is the first to provide mandatory token-level citations with executable verification rules for 3D CT report generation.
On NLG metrics, \method{} (B2\textquotesingle v2) achieves 0.467 BLEU-4 and 0.626 ROUGE-L on CT-RATE, and 0.506 BLEU-4 and 0.680 ROUGE-L on RadGenome.
We emphasize that these scores are obtained under a sentence-prompted setting where the planner uses reference sentences as generation topics, and hence represent controlled upper bounds.
The evaluation protocols also differ across methods (published splits for baselines vs.\ our standardized split for \method{}), so direct NLG comparison should be interpreted with this caveat.
The primary contribution of \method{} is the auditable evidence interface, not NLG score improvement.

\subsubsection{Ablation Study}
\label{sec:ablation}

To isolate the marginal contribution of each pipeline component, we design a seven-configuration ablation chain in which each successive configuration adds exactly one module.
Table~\ref{tab:ablation} reports the results on the full 5K test set (3{,}979 sentences from 496 patients).

\noindent\textbf{Routing-only configurations (A0--E1).}
In the routing-only regime (no LLM generation), the ``sentence text'' is the reference topic itself, and violations arise solely from misalignment between the topic's spatial claims and the routed tokens.
The baseline A0 (identity $\mathbf{W}_{\mathrm{proj}}$ + anatomy-primary routing) yields Viol\%$=$7.59\% [95\% CI: 6.84, 8.35], with all 237 violations concentrated in R3 (depth).
Training $\mathbf{W}_{\mathrm{proj}}$ (A0$\to$A1) reduces Viol\% to 6.01\% ($p_{\mathrm{adj}}{<}0.01$), a statistically significant improvement driven by fewer depth mismatches (R3: 237$\to$174).
Switching to spatial-filter semantic-rerank routing (A1$\to$E1) further halves R3 from 174 to 86, but introduces 95 new R1 (laterality) violations.
This trade-off arises because semantic reranking promotes tokens with high text--visual similarity regardless of their spatial side, occasionally routing opposite-side tokens when the semantic signal is strong but spatially misleading.

\noindent\textbf{LLM generation with evidence card (B2\textquotesingle--B2\textquotesingle v2).}
Adding LLM generation with Evidence Card~v1 (E1$\to$B2\textquotesingle) slightly reduces R6b cross-sentence conflicts (65$\to$44) as the LLM produces more coherent text than raw reference topics, but R1 rises to 109.
The key result is the B2\textquotesingle$\to$B2\textquotesingle v2 transition: Evidence Card~v2 (strict laterality gate with $\rho_t{\geq}0.9$, minimum 2 non-cross tokens, plus opposite-side and out-of-range token cleanup) reduces Viol\% from 6.01\% to \textbf{4.25\%} [3.60, 4.83], the largest single-step improvement in the chain ($p_{\mathrm{adj}}{=}0.006$).
R3 drops sharply from 86 to 23, confirming that the depth cleanup step effectively removes out-of-range tokens before they can influence generation.
R1 decreases from 109 to 98, consistent with the stricter laterality gate preventing ambiguous side claims.

\noindent\textbf{Post-hoc verification (C2\textquotesingle--D2).}
The LLM judge (C2\textquotesingle) and repair executor (D2), which branch from B2\textquotesingle{} with Evidence Card~v1, yield Viol\% of 6.18\% and 5.96\% respectively---both \emph{higher} than B2\textquotesingle v2.
The C2\textquotesingle regression is statistically significant ($p_{\mathrm{adj}}{=}0.006$ vs.\ B2\textquotesingle v2): the judge occasionally re-routes tokens into configurations that introduce new violations.
This result demonstrates that constructive evidence gating (preventing errors at generation time) is more effective than post-hoc correction (detecting and repairing errors after generation).

\noindent\textbf{NLG stability.}
Across all four generation configurations (B2\textquotesingle, B2\textquotesingle v2, C2\textquotesingle, D2), BLEU-4 varies within [0.471, 0.477], ROUGE-L is constant at 0.643, and METEOR ranges from 0.618 to 0.622.
The maximal BLEU-4 difference is less than 0.006 ($<$1.3\%), confirming that stricter evidence gating does not degrade generation quality.

\subsubsection{Spatial Grounding and Generalization}
\label{sec:grounding}

Table~\ref{tab:grounding} provides a fine-grained evaluation of spatial grounding quality and citation faithfulness, computed on 564 grounding-eligible sentences (bilateral cases excluded to avoid ambiguous laterality evaluation).

\noindent\textbf{Anatomy overlap and hit rate.}
The anatomy-primary routing baselines (A0, A1) achieve perfect scores across all spatial metrics (overlap$=$0.931, Hit@1$=$100\%, routing accuracy$=$100\%) by construction: IoU directly controls token selection, ensuring all cited tokens fall within the target anatomy region.
The semantic-rerank configurations (E1 and beyond) trade raw overlap for semantic relevance: mean overlap decreases to 0.685 and Hit@1 drops to 76.8\%.
However, Hit@8 remains at 98--99\%, confirming that at least one well-localized token is consistently present in the routed set even when semantic reranking promotes semantically relevant but spatially peripheral tokens.

\noindent\textbf{Citation and depth faithfulness.}
Evidence Card~v2 (B2\textquotesingle v2) improves citation faithfulness from 76.9\% (v1) to 77.6\% and depth faithfulness from 97.3\% to 98.4\%.
These gains are attributable to the strict cleanup step: removing opposite-side and out-of-range tokens before generation ensures that the Evidence Card constraints presented to the LLM more accurately reflect the cleaned citation set.
Interestingly, the post-hoc judge (C2\textquotesingle) achieves the highest CF of 81.1\%.
This is because the LLM judge selectively identifies and repairs the most egregious laterality mismatches, directly improving the agreement between generated text and cited positions.
However, this CF gain comes at the cost of additional LLM forward passes (4{,}112 vs.\ 3{,}979) and a regression in overall violation rate (Table~\ref{tab:ablation}), reinforcing our finding that constructive gating is preferable to post-hoc repair in the aggregate.

\noindent\textbf{Laterality accuracy.}
All configurations achieve laterality accuracy above 96.7\%, indicating that the vast majority of lateralised sentences are correctly supported by same-side tokens.
The anatomy-primary baselines (A0, A1) reach 100\% by design.
B2\textquotesingle v2 maintains 97.1\%, with the remaining 2.9\% attributable to cases where the semantic reranking selects tokens near the midline that are classified as opposite-side by the strict tolerance band ($\delta{=}0$).

\subsubsection{Qualitative Analysis}
\label{sec:qualitative}

Figure~\ref{fig:case_study} presents a representative closed-loop audit trace from the test set (case \texttt{train\_12976\_b\_1}, CT-RATE, sentence~4), illustrating the three-stage error detection and repair process.

In the initial generation (Stage~3c with B2\textquotesingle{} configuration), the LLM produces the sentence: \emph{``irregular opacity was observed in the left lung lower lobe.''}
The routed citation set contains 8 tokens, of which 6 are located on the right side of the midline and only 2 on the left (same-side ratio$=$0.25 for the ``left'' claim).
\textsc{CLEAR}-6 (Stage~4) triggers an R1 laterality violation: the sentence claims ``left'' but the dominant evidence is right-sided.

The LLM judge (Stage~5a) receives the sentence text, the Evidence Card (showing right dominance), and the R1 violation details.
It confirms the violation and identifies the offending span (``left lung''), suggesting a \texttt{drop\_laterality} repair action with the reasoning that the evidence is right-dominant and the left claim is unsupported.

The repair executor (Stage~5b) applies the suggested action by stripping the laterality keyword from the sentence, producing: \emph{``irregular opacity was observed in the lung lower lobe.''}
The repaired sentence is re-verified against \textsc{CLEAR}-6 and passes all rules, as no laterality claim remains to conflict with the right-dominant evidence.

This example demonstrates three properties of the constructive citation interface: (1)~spatial errors that would be invisible in a global-prefix system are \emph{deterministically detectable} because each sentence commits to a specific token set; (2)~the structured Evidence Card provides sufficient information for an LLM judge to \emph{diagnose the root cause} (right-dominant evidence vs.\ left claim); and (3)~targeted repair actions can \emph{surgically correct} the error without regenerating the entire report.
